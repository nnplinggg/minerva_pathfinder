# Problem Statement: Minerva Pathfinder

## Problem Framing Narrative

**I am:** An admitted or freshman Minerva student who is ambitious but feeling overwhelmed by the complexity of the curriculum and the uncertainty of my future path.
- Feeling the pressure of making the "right" choice for my career.
- Overwhelmed by dense, text-heavy information about courses.
- Searching for a community that shares my specific interests.

**Trying to:**
- Understand how Minerva's curriculum maps to my interests and discover a supportive network of peers and alumni.

**But:**
- I am faced with "walls of text" and administrative lists that don't tell a story or show a path.
- Information is fragmented and lacks interactive exploration.
- It's hard to find and connect with the right people who have "been there."
- There's no clear bridge between my interests and the available courses/network.

**Because:**
- The current information delivery is optimized for administrative clarity rather than student discovery and emotional "fit."

**Which makes me feel:**
- Uncertain, disconnected, and anxious about my decision or my academic trajectory.

## Context & Constraints
- Students are often remote or in different time zones during the decision phase.
- Mobile-first access is critical for Gen Z users.
- Static-only hosting (GitHub Pages) means we must rely on client-side logic and static data.

## Final Problem Statement
- Admitted and freshman students need a personalized, interactive way to explore Minerva's curriculum and network because the current text-heavy information prevents them from finding their "fit" and connecting with a supportive community.
