# Problem Framing Canvas: Minerva Pathfinder

## Look Inward
- **Problem (symptoms):** Admitted and freshman students are overwhelmed by "walls of text" when trying to understand Minerva's curriculum and how it aligns with their interests. This leads to uncertainty about "fit" and difficulty navigating career paths.
- **Why unsolved:** Existing information is likely static, dense, and organized by administrative categories (courses, majors) rather than student interests or career trajectories.
- **Our role/biases:** We might assume that students already know how to navigate complex academic structures or that text-heavy descriptions are sufficient for decision-making.

## Look Outward
- **Who experiences it:** Admitted students who haven't committed yet; freshman students starting their academic journey; parents supporting these students.
- **When/where:** During the decision-making period after admission; during the first year of study when choosing courses and exploring career paths.
- **Consequences:** Lower yield rate (admitted students not committing); student anxiety; misalignment between student interests and academic choices.
- **Who else/Who not:** Prospective students (pre-admission) might also feel this, but the focus is on those already admitted or enrolled. Excludes staff who already understand the system.
- **Who benefits:** Students and parents benefit from clarity and connection. The University benefits from higher yield and better-oriented students.

## Reframe
- **Problem restated:** Students lack a personalized, interactive way to map their passions to Minerva's unique educational model and community.
- **How Might We:** How might we transform Minerva's dense curriculum and network into an interactive discovery engine that fosters a sense of belonging and purpose?
